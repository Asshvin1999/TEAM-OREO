Corona Virus Symptoms

It’s time to say “Namaste” to the exceptionally vigorous symptoms of the resilient pandemic COVID-19.

Coughing And Sneezing

Coronavirus spreads from person to person mainly in aerosol droplets that flee when you cough or sneeze. These tiny droplets move through the air and spread the virus.

Hot Fever

A fever is the body’s way of warning you that something is off and can be a sign that your body is trying to fight an illness or infection. For COVID-19, a high fever can be a presenting symptom, however there are other pathogens to consider if fever is the only symptom.

Strong Headache

Headache is considered a primary symptom of COVID-19. A study showed that approximately 13% of participants experienced headache.

Shortness Of Breath

Clinically known as dyspnea, shortness of breath is one of the hallmark symptoms of COVID-19. This symptom can persist and quickly escalates in people with the disease.

Confusion

Physicians around the world have documented neurological symptoms in a significant fraction of COVID-19 patients. Some patients have experienced headache, dizziness and other relatively minor symptoms, while others have had more serious problems like confusion, impaired movement, seizures and strokes.

Sore Throat

A sore throat isn’t a very common COVID-19 symptom. A study in China commissioned by the WHO, found that out of more than 55,000 confirmed cases, only 13.9 percent of people reported a sore throat.

How Virus Spread

How Contagion Coronavirus

Human Contact

It’s time to bid adieu to hugs, handshakes and any form of human contact. Thanks to the novel Coronavirus, direct physical contact is a no.

Air Transmission

Masks masks masks, wear masks! Masks have become an essential part of our lives ever since the onset of this pandemic. Air transmission as a matter of fact happens to be the easiest means to contract the disease.

Contaminated Objects

This is an indirect form of transmission of the coronavirus where a person who comes in contact with objects or articles which could be potential carriers of coronavirus, may contract the disease.

How To Protect Yourself

Stay Safe From The Coronavirus.

Avoid Close Contact

Abide by this rule to reduce the risk of infection.

Clean Your Hands Often

Wash your hands with soap and water as many times as possible. When this option isn’t viable, sanitizer acts as an excellent substitute.

Stay At Home

Inheriting the art of “social distancing” is of prime importance. Please stay indoors as much as possible.

Cover Coughs And Sneezes

This is not only a major etiquette to be acquired but it also acts as an essential measure to steer clear of spreading the coronavirus.

Wear a Facemask if You Are Sick

“Prevention is better than cure” is one of the most common proverbs we’ve heard. Wear a face mask especially when sick, to prevent the spread of pathogens.

Clean And Disinfect

Contaminated objects are one of the major reasons for the proliferation of the number of infected people. Sanitization and disinfection is a vital precaution to be taken by all.
